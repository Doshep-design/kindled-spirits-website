The Issue

What is SENIOR LONELINESS?

Kindled Spirits believes this conversation matters because isolation can quietly affect mental health, physical health, and a person’s sense of belonging.

By Kindled Spirits

An Overlooked Burden

“some older adults — especially those dealing with major physical health or mental health issues — still have much higher rates of loneliness and social isolation than others”
Kara Gavin, Michigan Medicine
★ ★ ★ ★ ★

“With a greater connectivity between technology and a larger divide in social activity, senior citizens are feeling more isolated than ever, both in terms of family and technology.”

Why is senior loneliness so common?

As people get older, they lack the ability to meet others, both physically and mentally, as with age comes weakness and disability, potentially preventing interaction with seniors and their families.

In addition, many seniors may feel emotionally disconnected from their families, and with no real support or bonds with anyone, they feel isolated.

✦ ✦ ✦ ✦

What can we do?

  • As a community, you can reach out to seniors you know and comfort them.
  • Make calls with your parents or grandparents, and try to make cards or messages for seniors.
  • By making seniors feel loved as a community, we can reduce senior loneliness world-wide.
